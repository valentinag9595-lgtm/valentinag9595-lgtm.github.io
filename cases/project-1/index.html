Problem

Our subscription page wasn't effective, because users mostly picked the weekly plan. The yearly option wasn't clear or attractive, so people didn't see the value.

Insight

From past data, we saw that when both options were shown in a simpler side-by-side layout, more users chose the yearly plan.

Goal

We aimed to redesign the page to make the yearly benefits clearer, simplify the choice, and guide more users toward the annual subscription.

Key Task

The new subscription screen needed to drive more users to the annual plan (dpCVR annual). At the same time, it was important not to harm overall purchases (pCVR) or trial starts (tCVR). Our goal was a 10–15% uplift by making the options clearer and easier to understand.

Add annotated image

Competitors Research

Analyzed top-grossing apps in the same category. Most successful paywalls used a side-by-side plan comparison with clear savings callout and a highlighted recommended option.

Hypothesis

Hypothesis 1: Showing plans side-by-side with a visible savings badge will increase yearly plan selection.

Hypothesis 2: Moving the free trial label to a more prominent position will increase overall trial starts without hurting annual conversions.

Design Options

We explored 4 design directions, each testing a different layout and messaging approach. Options were reviewed in a design workshop with the product and growth team.

Key Decisions

— Prioritised the yearly plan visibility above the fold

— Added a side-by-side layout with a "Save 60%" badge

— Kept the free trial CTA prominent to protect tCVR

Add design options image

Implementation

A/B test launched March 18–27, 2025. Built entirely in a no-code builder — shipped without any engineering resources. Equal traffic split across all variants.

Impact

The winning variant outperformed the control across all key metrics, with no negative effect on overall purchases or trial starts.
